Scheme Name: PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana
Category: Agriculture
Description: Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi is a central government scheme that provides direct income support to small and marginal farmer families across India. The scheme was launched on 24th February 2019. Under this scheme, the government transfers Rs 6,000 per year in three equal installments of Rs 2,000 each directly into the bank accounts of eligible farmers. The aim is to supplement the financial needs of farmers for purchasing inputs related to agriculture and allied activities.
Eligibility: All landholding farmer families with cultivable land are eligible. The family should include husband, wife, and minor children. Benefits: Rs 6,000 per year transferred directly to bank account in three installments of Rs 2,000 each. First installment: April to July. Second installment: August to November. Third installment: December to March.
Documents Required: Aadhaar card, bank account details (account number and IFSC code), land ownership records, mobile number linked with Aadhaar.
How to Apply: Farmers can register through the official PM Kisan portal at pmkisan.gov.in. They can also visit their nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) for registration. State government nodal officers can also help with registration. After registration, verification is done at the state and district level.

Documents Required: Aadhaar card or ration card for identity verification. SECC 2011 database listing is mandatory. Any government-issued photo ID.

Scheme Name: Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)
Category: Rural Employment
Description: MGNREGA is a landmark legislation enacted on 25th August 2005 that guarantees 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. The scheme provides a legal guarantee of work, making it a demand-driven program. If work is not provided within 15 days of demand, an unemployment allowance must be paid. The scheme focuses on creating durable assets like roads, canals, ponds, wells, and other rural infrastructure. The wages are paid directly to the worker's bank account.
Eligibility: Any adult member of a rural household willing to do unskilled manual work. The household must reside in a rural area. The applicant must be above 18 years of age. The household must apply for a Job Card at the Gram Panchayat. One Job Card is issued per household and it contains photographs of all adult members who are willing to work.
Benefits: Guaranteed 100 days of wage employment per financial year per household. Current wage rate varies by state (around Rs 250-350 per day depending on the state). Wages paid within 15 days of work through bank account or post office account. Work is provided within 5 km of the applicant's residence. If work is not provided within 15 days, an unemployment allowance is paid. Worksite facilities like drinking water, shade, and first aid must be provided.
Documents Required: Aadhaar card, passport size photograph, proof of rural residence, bank account or post office account details, filled Job Card application form.
How to Apply: Apply for a Job Card at the nearest Gram Panchayat office. The Gram Panchayat verifies the application and issues a Job Card within 15 days. Once the Job Card is received, submit a written application demanding work to the Gram Panchayat. Work must be provided within 15 days of the demand.
Official Website: https://nrega.nic.in
Helpline: 1800-345-22-44 (toll-free) or contact Gram Panchayat directly

Scheme Name: Digital India - Common Service Centres (CSC)
Category: Digital Services / E-Governance
Description: Common Service Centres (CSCs) are the access points for delivery of essential public utility services, social welfare schemes, healthcare, financial, education, and agriculture services to citizens in rural and remote areas of India. CSCs are a part of the Digital India programme and operate as physical facilities for digital access. There are over 5 lakh CSCs across India. CSCs provide a wide range of government-to-citizen (G2C) and business-to-citizen (B2C) services. Citizens can use CSCs to access various government scheme registrations, certificate applications, and digital services.
Eligibility: Any Indian citizen can use the services of Common Service Centres. CSCs are available across India in rural and urban areas. Village Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) operate CSCs and provide assisted digital services to citizens who may not have internet access or digital literacy.
Benefits: Access to government scheme applications: Aadhaar enrollment, PM Kisan registration, Ayushman Bharat card, pension scheme registration, etc. Banking and financial services: Aadhaar-based banking, insurance enrollment, pension services. Digital literacy training through PMGDISHA. Certificate services: Birth, death, caste, income, domicile certificates. Education services: Digital learning, skill development courses. Health services: Telemedicine, health card issuance. Bill payments and utility services.
Documents Required: Depends on the specific service being availed. Generally, Aadhaar card and other basic identity documents are required.
How to Apply: Visit the nearest CSC. You can locate the nearest CSC through the CSC locator at locator.csccloud.in. No prior appointment is needed. The VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) at the CSC will assist with the required service.
Official Website: https://csc.gov.in
Helpline: 1800-121-3468 (toll-free)

Scheme Name: Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY)
Category: Food Security
Description: Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana provides free foodgrains to approximately 81.35 crore beneficiaries covered under the National Food Security Act (NFSA). Originally launched in March 2020 as a relief measure during COVID-19, the scheme has been extended and made a regular programme. Under this scheme, every eligible beneficiary gets 5 kg of rice or wheat free of cost per month. This is in addition to the subsidized rations already available under NFSA through the Public Distribution System (PDS). The scheme ensures food security for the poorest sections of society.
Eligibility: Beneficiaries must be covered under the National Food Security Act (NFSA) 2013. This includes Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households and Priority Household (PHH) cardholders. The beneficiary must possess a valid ration card (AAY or PHH category). Eligibility is determined by the state government based on socio-economic criteria.
Benefits: 5 kg of free foodgrains (rice or wheat) per person per month for Priority Household cardholders. 35 kg per household per month for Antyodaya Anna Yojana households. The foodgrains are distributed through the Public Distribution System (PDS) at Fair Price Shops. Aadhaar-based biometric authentication ensures transparency.
Documents Required: Valid ration card (AAY or PHH category), Aadhaar card for biometric authentication at the Fair Price Shop.
How to Apply: No separate application is needed for PMGKAY. If you have a valid ration card under NFSA, you are automatically eligible. Visit your designated Fair Price Shop with your ration card. Authenticate using Aadhaar-based biometric verification. Collect your free monthly foodgrains. To get a new ration card, apply at the nearest Food and Civil Supplies Department or through the state's online PDS portal.
Official Website: https://nfsa.gov.in
Helpline: Contact state Food and Civil Supplies Department or 1967 (PDS helpline in many states)

Scheme Name: PM YASASVI - Yet Another Scheme for Aspiring Students from Vulnerable Indian Sections
Category: Education / Scholarship
Description: PM YASASVI is a scholarship scheme by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment for students from OBC, EBC (Economically Backward Classes), DNT (Denotified Tribes), and Semi-Nomadic and Nomadic Tribes (SNT) communities. The scheme provides scholarships for students studying in Classes 9 to 12 in recognized schools across India. The scholarship aims to support the education of students from vulnerable sections of society who face financial constraints. The scholarship is awarded based on a national-level entrance test conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).
Eligibility: Students belonging to OBC, EBC, DNT, SNT communities. Studying in Class 9 or Class 11 in a government or recognized private school. Family annual income should not exceed Rs 2.5 lakh. Students should not be availing any other scholarship. Age criteria: 15-17 years for Class 9 and 17-19 years for Class 11 (as on specific date mentioned in the notification).
Benefits: Rs 75,000 per year for students in Class 9 and 10. Rs 1,25,000 per year for students in Class 11 and 12. The scholarship covers tuition fees, hostel fees, and other educational expenses. Directly transferred to the student's bank account.
Documents Required: Aadhaar card, community certificate (OBC/EBC/DNT/SNT), income certificate (annual income below Rs 2.5 lakh), school enrollment proof, bank account details, passport size photograph, mark sheet of the previous class.
How to Apply: Register and apply online through the National Scholarship Portal at scholarships.gov.in or the NTA website when the application window opens (usually around July-August). Take the PM YASASVI entrance test conducted by NTA. Merit list is prepared based on the test scores. Selected students receive the scholarship directly in their bank accounts.
Official Website: https://yet.nta.ac.in and https://scholarships.gov.in
Helpline: NTA Helpline - 011-40759000
